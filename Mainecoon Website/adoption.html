Home Care Adoptions

Before You Adopt

Bringing home a Maine Coon is a long-term commitment—these friendly, gentle cats can live 12–15+ years. Make sure your home, routine, and budget support a large, social, people-oriented companion. Maine Coons thrive with daily interaction, regular play, and consistent grooming.

  • Time & attention: daily play and enrichment; they don’t love being ignored.
  • Grooming: weekly brushing (2–3× is ideal) to manage that luxurious coat.
  • Space & gear: tall scratching post, sturdy cat tree, and wide litter box.
  • Budget: food, litter, toys, vet care, and a little extra for the unexpected.

Where to Adopt

You can find wonderful Maine Coons and Maine-Coon-mixes through rescues, local shelters, and responsible breeders. Be wary of deals that seem too good to be true— prioritize health, transparency, and welfare.

  • Rescue & Shelter: ask about temperament, medical history, and foster notes.
  • Responsible breeder: health testing, contracts, and lifetime take-back are good signs.
  • Networking: local cat clubs and vets may know of upcoming surrenders or litters.

How the Process Works

  1. Apply: share your home setup, schedule, and experience with pets.
  2. Meet & greet: visit in person; look for calm curiosity and comfortable interaction.
  3. Approve & prepare: gather supplies—carrier, litter setup, food, bowls, and a tall post.
  4. Adopt: sign paperwork, pay fees, and schedule a prompt vet visit.
  5. Decompress: give a quiet room for the first few days and introduce slowly to other pets.

Home-Ready Checklist

  • Carrier (Large/XL), wide litter box, and unscented clumping litter.
  • Food & water bowls; consider a circulating fountain.
  • High-protein food per vet guidance; have a small supply ready.
  • Tall scratching post (≥ 36″) and a sturdy cat tree.
  • Grooming kit: wide-tooth comb, undercoat rake, slicker brush, nail clippers.
  • Enrichment: wand toys, puzzle feeders, kicker toys, and window perch.

Typical Costs

Adoption fees vary by organization. Expect ongoing costs for quality food, litter, toys, and routine veterinary care. Planning a monthly pet budget keeps surprises small.

Many new owners also set aside a modest emergency fund for unexpected vet visits or supplies. A little planning up front makes the transition easier on both you and your new companion.

Ready to Start?

If a friendly, easygoing companion with a big heart sounds right for you, begin your journey today. Reach out to a rescue or shelter, or talk with a reputable breeder. Your future Maine Coon is waiting!

Take your time, ask lots of questions, and make sure the match fits your household and routine. A great adoption sets you up for a long, happy life together.
